# Summary of project

The core component of the project was to investigate and utilize penalization methods to 
create sparse statistical models. A sparse model--one which uses only a subset of all
predictors--is less complex and more interpretable. The Ridge, Lasso and Group Lasso 
penalization methods were applied on models to try to predict Parkison's disease from 
a subset of all available predictors in the dataset. Ultimately, the goal was to find 
which predictors play a significant role in predicting the disease. In this case the 
dataset had 18 predictors. In the end it was shown that by using  penalization, and by 
adjusting the coefficient of penalization, the sparsity of the models could be controled. 
The sparser models were more interpretable without loosing predictive power.
